Unwrapping the Banana Tree: Facts, Uses & Farming

Growing a banana tree takes around 10 months. From one matured tree, new banana shoots (called suckers) naturally grow, continuing the cycle. It’s important not to use sea water for irrigation. Instead, use clean well water or canal water (sottu water) for healthy growth. Each plant should be spaced with at least a 1-meter gap between them to allow proper sunlight, airflow, and nutrient intake.

Banana trees aren't actually trees—they're the world’s largest herb! Each plant can produce fruit just once, after which a new shoot takes over. Their leaves are used in eco-friendly plates, traditional cooking, and temple rituals. NASA once studied banana plants for space farming due to their fast growth and utility!

The banana tree is a living gift that gives food, shade, and new life—all from one stem..

The outer part of the stem is used for making ropes or mats. Even the banana skin is useful — it can be used as fertilizer or compost. After harvesting, the main plant is cut, but small baby plants grow again from the side. In this way, the banana tree keeps growing. Every part of the banana tree — leaf, fruit, flower, stem, and even peel — is useful.
